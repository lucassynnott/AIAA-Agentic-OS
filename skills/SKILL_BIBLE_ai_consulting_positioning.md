# SKILL BIBLE: AI Consulting Positioning (5-10x Revenue)

## Executive Summary

This skill bible documents positioning and framing strategies that generate 5-10x more revenue for delivering the exact same product with only ~10% more effort. The core shift: from "builder" positioning to "strategic partner" positioning.

**Core Insight:** "When you sell workflows, you are inherently framing yourself as basically being a technician—somebody working inside the business almost as an employee. With employee-level expectations surrounding your compensation."

Three levers control premium positioning: Positioning (consultant vs builder), Experience (human touchpoints), and Risk (guarantees and alignment).

---

## 1. The Builder vs Partner Framework

### The Builder Trap

**When you sell workflows**:
- You're viewed as a technician
- Working "inside" the business as pseudo-employee
- Employee-level compensation expectations
- Someone hands you spec → you build → you deliver JSON/blueprint

**When you sell outcomes**:
- You're viewed as a partner
- Business owner turns to you for high-level problems
- Partner-level compensation expectations
- You identify problems → recommend solutions → implement strategically

### Dave vs Serena Example

**Dave the Developer** (Technical Lead):
"I can build you an AI lead qualification system. It'll use custom webhooks and multi-branch conditional logic that will automatically score your prospects based on a set of predefined criteria."

**Serena the Strategist** (Outcome Lead):
"I help companies double their topline revenue by doubling the number of qualified leads that come in on the front end. I do this using AI lead qualification."

### What Each Gets Paid For

| Person | Paid For | Incentive |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| Dave | Complexity of build | Make builds as complex as possible |
| Serena | What she delivers | Make solutions as simple as possible |

**Result**: Serena saves time with simple solutions AND justifies more money.

### The $20K Question
"How much would you be willing to spend to double your revenue?"

If client makes $20,000/month and Serena says "I'll make you another $20,000/month"—they'll pay a significant fraction of that.

## 2. The Early Mistake

### Technical Enthusiasm Trap
Common pattern when starting out:
- Just learned AI/automation
- Excited about technology
- Want to share with the world

**What happens on calls**:
- Client: "We suffer from X thing"
- You: Start talking about all technical ways to solve X
- "We could integrate your CRM with email platform using make.com"
- "We could set up advanced segmentation based on user behavior"
- "We could connect X, Y, and Z APIs"

**Result**: $1,500 average ticket price (front-end)

### The Realization
"The client didn't give a shit about HOW I would do the thing. The client cares about the WHAT and the WHY."

If you sell the HOW, you position yourself as someone who doesn't get the game.

### The Fix
**Before** (Client: "We suffer from X"):
"We can build you an N8N workflow that does X, Y, and Z."

**After** (Client: "We suffer from X"):
"Wow, that must be costing you a lot of money. Tell me more about that."

## 3. Consultant-First Positioning

### The Identity Shift

| Old Identity | New Identity |
|--------------|--------------|
| Builder who sells services | Consultant who happens to build |
| Technical implementer | Strategic advisor with implementation skills |
| Vendor | Partner |

### Why This Is More Dangerous (From Client Perspective)
You're not just capable of implementing—you're capable of bringing strategic insights to improve their business, just like a fellow business partner would.

### The Upsell Effect
When positioned as strategic partner:
- Client brings OTHER problems to you
- "While you're here, what do you think about our customer retention?"
- "Do you have thoughts on how to generate more leads?"
- You're not just "the automation person"
- You're "their strategy person"

### Advanced Opportunities
Strategic positioning enables:
- Revenue shares
- Equity deals
- Long-term retainers
- Advisory board positions

## 4. The Experience Factor (H&M vs Burberry)

### The $25 vs $500 Shirt

**H&M**: $25 shirt
- Same materials as Burberry
- Commodity product
- Transactional purchase

**Burberry**: $500 shirt
- Same materials as H&M
- Prestige experience
- Relationship purchase

### The Burberry Experience
1. **Lineup at door**: Limits people, adds perceived demand
2. **Personal greeting**: Offered drink (tea, brandy)
3. **Personal style specialist assigned**: "Where are you from? What do you like?"
4. **Complimentary measurements**: Free service, builds relationship
5. **Amazing environment**: Smell, beautiful staff, luxury aesthetic
6. **Constant greasing of wheels**: Make customer feel privileged

**Key insight**: "Their value proposition is not in the clothes. It's everything around the clothes—how privileged can we make customers feel."

## 5. Applying Experience to AI Consulting

### The Automation Trap
Early mistake: Try to automate everything
- Client onboarding → Automated
- Project updates → Automated
- Invoicing → Automated

**Result**: 
- Clients felt like dealing with a machine
- Questions/concerns routed to automated systems
- Very poor retention
- Very few upsells
- 3-4x less per customer
- Poor LTV

### The White Glove Solution

**Strategic touchpoints**:
- Customized videos (from you personally)
- Updates Monday, Wednesday, Friday
- Personalized kickoff calls (not forms)
- Guide them through the process

**The Video Hack**:
Record lots of videos for deliverables
- Provides illusion of FaceTime
- Without sunk cost of synchronous communication
- Scales better than live calls

### The Hidden Automation
"We still automate the hell out of stuff behind the scenes."

Key rule: **Don't automate anything the client personally experiences.**

Give them:
- Perception of white glove service
- Peace of mind that you're taking care of everything
- Confidence they're getting premium treatment

## 6. Where to Focus Effort

### The Skill Trap
"Knowing what it takes to fulfill your service is important. You need to know how to do the thing."

**But**: Doing the thing is NOT what dramatically increases value.

### The 10% Rule
If you know how to do the thing, you're ahead of 90% of service providers.

That's enough technical competence.

### Where Extra Effort Belongs

| Area | Impact on Value |
|------|-----------------|
| Sales process | High |
| Aesthetic quality of deliverables | High |
| Onboarding sequence | High |
| Technical implementation details | Low |

Focus additional time/energy on packaging, presentation, and delivery—not technical depth.

## 7. The Risk Lever (Guarantees)

### Why Risk Matters Last
To use guarantees effectively, you must first understand:
1. Positioning (selling outcomes, not workflows)
2. Experience (human touchpoints)

Only then does risk mitigation work.

### Why Workflows Can't Be Guaranteed
Workflows are features. You can't guarantee features meaningfully.
- "I guarantee 15 nodes in your N8N flow" → Meaningless
- Customers don't care about nodes

### Why Outcomes Can Be Guaranteed
- "I guarantee 5% improvement to conversion rates in 30 days"
- "If it doesn't happen, I continue working free until it does"
- Specific, measurable, valuable to client

### The Guarantee Sales Hack
Guarantees increase top-of-funnel conversion by several hundred percentage points.

**Trade-off**:
- Top of funnel increase: ~300%
- Bottom line reduction (refunds): ~10%
- Net: 270% increase (2.7x revenue)

### Incentive Alignment
Guarantees are pre-commitment mechanisms:
- Obligate you to deliver promised outcome
- Two universes: guarantee vs no guarantee
- Which universe do you try harder in? Obviously the guarantee one.

**Result**: Improves probability of delivery. Literally free money.

## 9. Immediate Action Items

### Action 1: Shift to Outcomes
- Map technical capabilities to results
- Don't start from scratch
- Review all systems built for clients in last 1-2 years

**Create a sheet**:
For each system, ask: "What was the outcome this delivered?"
- Not the workflow
- The actual result on the business
- Lift to conversion rate, leads generated, dollars generated, profit improvement

**If missing data**: Ask past clients
"Hey, I'm curious, how is the system holding up? What did it end up doing for your reply rates/CTR/[metric]?"

**Result**: List of outcomes to sell future clients (case studies, portfolio pieces) in language they care about.

### Action 2: Upgrade Client Experience
Spend a weekend thinking through:
- Deliverable aesthetics
- Asset quality

**Simple upgrades**:
- Add client logo to top-right of Google Doc templates
- Part of SOP: New client → add their logo

**Client updates**:
- Monday, Wednesday, Friday updates
- Actionable or not
- 2-3 bullet points or long essay
- Increases probability of future sale by hundreds of percentage points

### Action 3: Create a Guarantee
Start simple if needed:
- "30-day satisfaction guarantee" (minimum viable)

Better options:
- "5% lift to conversion rate within 30 days or you don't pay"
- "20 booked sales appointments in 60 days or I work free until I hit it"

With outcomes + better experience, probability of refunds decreases significantly.

## 10. The Complete Positioning Stack

### Layer 1: What You Say
- Lead with outcomes, not features
- "I help companies [outcome] by [method]"
- Ask about problems, not requirements

### Layer 2: How They Experience You
- White glove touchpoints
- Personal videos
- Regular updates (M/W/F)
- Guided onboarding

### Layer 3: How You Remove Risk
- Specific guarantees
- Continue working until outcome achieved
- Performance-based options available

### The Compound Effect
Each layer multiplies the others:
- Outcome positioning × Great experience × Risk removal = Premium pricing
- 5-10x revenue for same deliverable
- ~10% more effort

## AI PARSING GUIDE

### Primary Objective
When user asks about pricing, positioning, or how to charge more, guide them through the three levers: Positioning (outcomes), Experience (human touch), and Risk (guarantees).

### Critical Decision Points

**If User Says**: "I want to build X automation"
**Action Sequence**:
1. Ask: What outcome does this create for the client?
2. Reframe from "build X" to "achieve Y outcome"
3. Help quantify the value of Y
4. Structure guarantee around Y

**If User Says**: "Client says I'm too expensive"
**Action Sequence**:
1. Check: Are they positioned as builder or partner?
2. Check: What's the client experience like?
3. Check: Is there a guarantee?
4. Fix weakest of the three levers
