## Section 2: The LTV:CAC Mathematics (Foundation Layer)

### The 10X Rule That Changes Everything

**The Universal Formula:**

```
Lifetime Gross Profit : Cost to Acquire Customer = Minimum 10X
```

**Why Most Agencies Fail This:**

**Typical Bad Scenario (Won't Scale):**

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Ad Spend (or time equivalent) | $30,000/month |
| Cost Per Qualified Call | $300 |
| Close Rate | 15% |
| Monthly Price | $3,000 |
| Gross Profit Margin | 70% |
| Churn | 20% (keep 5 months) |
| **Lifetime Gross Profit** | **$10,500** |
| **CAC** | **$2,000** |
| **LTV:CAC Ratio** | **5.25X** ❌ |
| **Result** | **$127K profit** |

**The Problem:**
"You might think that's good - $2,000 to make $10,500. That is actually completely wrong. That is not even remotely close to what you need to be at to scale this company. This spread between these two numbers is too low. It needs to be a MINIMUM of 10."

**Correct Scenario (Scales Sustainably):**

| Metric | Value | Change |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Ad Spend | $30,000/month | Same |
| Cost Per Qualified Call | $450 | +$150 (targeting richer people) |
| Close Rate | 30% | +15% (rich people convert better) |
| Monthly Price | $3,300 | +$300 (rich people pay more) |
| Gross Profit Margin | 70% | Same |
| Churn | 10% | -10% (rich people stay 2x longer) |
| **Lifetime Gross Profit** | **$23,100** | **+120%** |
| **CAC** | **$2,400** | **+20%** |
| **LTV:CAC Ratio** | **9.6X → 10X+** ✅ |
| **Result** | **$432K profit** | **+240%** |

**The Triple Amplification Effect:**
1. Close rate goes UP (15% → 30%)
2. Price goes UP ($3K → $3.3K+)
3. Churn goes DOWN (20% → 10%)

**The Hidden Fourth Amplification:**
Show-up rate dramatically improves:
- Poor people: 50% show rate
- Rich people: 70-80% (sometimes 90%) show rate

## Section 3: Stop Selling to Poor People (The Foundational Rule)

### The Real Estate Mansion Analogy

**Fazio's Direct Challenge:**

"Let me ask you something. If you're trying to sell a $3,000/month service to a business, imagine that's the equivalent of trying to sell a $1.5-2 million mansion. Imagine you're a real estate agent. You know what happens when a real estate agent is selling a multi-million dollar home?

'Want to come see it? Proof of funds. Show proof of funds.'

I'm not going to get up off my ass and drive across the city, get the keys, walk them through the house, and do this multiple times for people who don't even have money."

### The Poor Client Virus

**What Happens When You Let a Poor Client In:**

**Scenario:** You have 5 clients
- 4 are fine
- 1 is poor

**That 1 poor client:**
- Gets terrible results (needs it to work desperately)
- Complains constantly
- Asks for refund
- Messages nonstop with problems
- Takes 80% of your mental energy

**The Psychological Destruction:**

"Now you're thinking in your head: 'Wow I'm really not good at what I do. I'm not good at all. I suck at this. I should just quit. I should switch my offer.'

This poor person is infesting the entire operation of your company and your psyche and your emotional stability and your entire life because you let this ONE poor person in as a client and they're literally ruining your life."

**What Poor Clients Destroy:**
1. Close percentage (harder to close)
2. Average revenue per customer (can't charge them much)
3. Gross profit margin (demand more for less)
4. Show-up rate (don't use calendars, irresponsible)
5. Churn (leave faster, complain more)
6. Your self-confidence ("I suck at this")
7. Overall enjoyment of life (constant stress)

### The Qualification System

**Implementation:**

**In Calendly/TypeForm (Pre-Qualification Questions):**
1. "What is your current monthly revenue?"
2. "What is your annual revenue?"
3. If below threshold → Auto-reject or don't offer calendar slots

**In Discovery Calls:**
1. First 3 minutes: "What's your current revenue?"
2. If they say "We're not comfortable sharing that" → End the call
3. Don't be scared to ask - it's rude to waste both parties' time

**The Non-Negotiable Rule:**

"When you start asking people how much money they make and you REJECT them, you need to reject the poor people and only sell to rich people."
