## Executive Summary

This comprehensive sales system, developed by Alex Hormozi through 13 years of experience and 4,000+ personal sales, represents a complete blueprint for maximizing sales performance in any business. The system is built on six foundational elements that prioritize what happens AROUND the sale (sales multipliers) as much as what happens DURING the sale itself.

The methodology challenges conventional sales wisdom by focusing heavily on operational excellence, systematic lead response, strategic scheduling, and team development. Rather than relying on charismatic closing techniques, this approach treats sales as a systematic, measurable process where small improvements in timing, availability, and follow-up can produce 300-400% increases in conversion rates. The framework is particularly powerful because it addresses the entire customer journey from initial interest through closing, with specific attention to show-up rates, lead response times, and team optimization. This holistic approach ensures that businesses don't just train better closers, but create better systems that make closing inevitable.

## Core Principles

### 1. Sales Multipliers Trump Sales Skills
The activities that happen AROUND the sale have more impact on revenue than what happens during the sale itself. A 391% increase in closing likelihood comes from responding within 60 seconds, while even exceptional sales training rarely produces such dramatic improvements. Focus on systematic improvements before skill development.

### 2. Speed of Response Is Everything
50% of prospects go with the business that responds first, not the best. The window of peak motivation is tiny - prospects have "huge motivation for a tiny period of time." When they knock on your door during this window, you must be ready to open it immediately.

### 3. Convenience Drives Conversion
Make it as easy as possible for prospects to buy when THEY want to buy, not when it's convenient for you. This means 7-day availability, same-day scheduling options, and multiple contact methods. Convenience often matters more than quality in the prospect's decision-making process.

### 4. Feed the Killers (Strategic Resource Allocation)
Your best leads should go to your best closers, and your worst leads should go to your worst closers. This minimizes waste and maximizes total revenue. A timeshare company increased revenue 5x by implementing this single change across their organization.

### 5. Show-Up Rates Matter More Than Close Rates
A 20% improvement in show-up rates often has the same revenue impact as a 20% improvement in close rates, but show-up rate improvements are typically much easier to achieve through systematic follow-up and reminder sequences.

### 6. Train Systems, Not Just Skills
Most sales organizations are "churn factories" that hope good people survive rather than systematically developing talent. Proper training systems allow you to hire below market rate and develop talent internally, creating sustainable competitive advantage.

### 7. Competition Should Be External, Not Internal
Create "us vs. them" mentality against external competitors rather than internal competition between team members. This builds cohesive teams while maintaining competitive drive.

### 8. The Sale Begins at First Contact
Every interaction from the first ad click through scheduling affects closing rates. Maintain consistent messaging and professionalism throughout the entire customer journey to create seamless experience.

### 9. Conviction Beats Technique
Salespeople who genuinely believe in the product will naturally use correct tonality and pacing. Reading testimonials and seeing customer results builds conviction more effectively than drilling scripts alone.

### 10. Analyze Success, Don't Just Fix Failures
When you're "hot" and closing at high rates, record and analyze everything to systematize what creates peak performance. Great closers never get cold because they've systematized their "hotness."

## Frameworks & Models

### The BANT Qualification Framework
**Origin:** IBM's comprehensive lead qualification study
**Components:**
- **Budget:** Does the prospect have money to spend?
- **Authority:** Can they make the decision?
- **Need:** Do they have the problem you solve?
- **Timing:** Do they want to start soon?

**Implementation:** Verify all four elements before presenting pricing. Can be done through applications, setter calls, or during the main sales conversation.

### The 60-Second Response System
**Step 1:** Set up automated systems to capture leads instantly
**Step 2:** Have live coverage during business hours for immediate response
**Step 3:** Train team on 60-second response protocols
**Step 4:** Track and measure response times religiously
**Step 5:** Provide "call now" options alongside scheduling links

**Key Metrics:**
- Under 60 seconds = 391% increase in close likelihood
- Over 5 minutes = 80% decrease in close likelihood
- First responder wins 50% of the time regardless of quality

### The 7-Day Sales Calendar Framework
**Structure:**
- Monday-Sunday availability (104 additional selling days per year)
- 15-minute scheduling increments for convenience
- Same-day/next-day priority booking
- Strategic off-hours coverage (5 PM - 8 PM for B2C)

**Implementation:**
1. Analyze when your prospects are most available
2. Adjust team schedules to match prospect availability
3. Use 15-minute increments but block 3-4 slots per appointment
4. Train "off-the-call" SOPs for non-appointment time
5. Pull forward Monday appointments to weekend same-day slots

### The Three-Touch Reminder Sequence
**Touch 1: 24 Hours Before**
- Manual, personalized message
- Show preparation work done for them
- Create obligation through demonstrated effort
- Example: "Set aside this t-shirt for you" or "Found 6 things to help your business"

**Touch 2: Morning Of**
- Brief reminder of afternoon appointment
- Confirm they remember and are planning to attend
- Keep it simple and direct

**Touch 3: 1 Hour Before**
- Final confirmation
- Use voice messages or video when possible
- Blue messages (iMessage) preferred over green (SMS)
- Brief and professional

### The Feed the Killers Lead Distribution System
**Tier 1 Closers:** Best qualified leads, highest budget prospects, warmest referrals
**Tier 2 Closers:** Medium qualified leads, standard budget prospects
**Tier 3 Closers:** Lowest qualified leads, price shoppers, difficult prospects

**Implementation Steps:**
1. Rank all salespeople by closing percentage and average deal size
2. Create lead scoring system based on qualification criteria
3. Automatically route highest scored leads to top performers
4. Use bottom tier as training ground for new salespeople
5. Promote salespeople to higher tiers based on performance metrics

### The Role-Playing Training Framework
**Setup Phase:**
- Set expectation: "No one's perfect, that's why we practice"
- Focus on ONE skill per session
- Announce what you're working on before starting

**Execution Phase:**
- Give real-time feedback (thumbs up, "great," nodding)
- Stop immediately when they mess up
- Have them repeat correct version 5-6 times in succession
- Work on same skill for 1-2 weeks until mastered

**Feedback Rules:**
- One piece of feedback at a time
- Immediate feedback (faster feedback = fewer repetitions needed)
- Positive reinforcement in real-time
- Expect 30-40 stops per script run-through

### The Game Tape Review System
**Weekly Sales + CS Joint Reviews:**
- Review actual recorded calls with both teams present
- Sales sees onboarding calls to understand customer journey
- CS sees sales calls to understand set expectations
- Identify disconnects between what's promised and delivered
- Create seamless handoff processes

**Individual Hot Streak Analysis:**
- Record everything when performing at peak
- Analyze tone, pacing, word choice, transitions
- Create checklist of "hotness" factors
- Systematize peak performance behaviors
- Focus on never getting cold vs. trying to get hot

- Focus on team-wide weakness
- Quick wins and challenges review
- Set daily activity goals

**Lead Response Protocol:**
- Monitor lead notifications constantly during business hours
- Have backup coverage for primary responders
- Use multiple contact methods (call, text, email)
- Log all response times and track metrics

**Scheduling Management:**
- Review next day appointments each evening
- Confirm all appointments 24 hours prior
- Pull forward distant appointments when slots open
- Maintain same-day/next-day booking priority

**Off-the-Call Activities:**
- Call upcoming appointments to qualify and pull forward
- Follow up on no-shows and reschedules

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Operational Mistakes

**The 9-5 Trap:** Only selling during traditional business hours when your prospects work those same hours. Your customers are available evenings and weekends - that's when they're thinking about their problems and have time to make decisions.

**The Monday Stack:** Skipping weekend availability and forcing all weekend interest into Monday appointments. This creates no-show disasters and missed opportunities when prospects are actually ready to buy.

**The Response Delay:** Thinking leads will wait for you to get organized. After 5 minutes, you lose 80% of your closing opportunity. After a day, you're essentially starting over with a cold lead.

**The Round Robin Fallacy:** Distributing leads equally regardless of closer skill level. This wastes your best opportunities on your worst performers and frustrates your top performers with impossible prospects.

**The Busy Calendar Illusion:** Making your calendar look busy by blocking fake appointments. This only makes it harder for prospects to find convenient times and doesn't impress anyone.

### Training Mistakes

**The Feedback Overload:** Giving multiple pieces of feedback at once. People can only improve one thing at a time, and delayed feedback requires exponentially more repetitions to stick.

**The Best Closer Manager:** Promoting your best closer to sales manager without considering whether they have teaching and leadership skills. Great players don't automatically make great coaches.

**The Competition Confusion:** Creating internal competition between team members instead of uniting them against external competitors. This destroys team cohesion and reduces total sales.

**The Script Perfectionism:** Focusing only on what to say without understanding why it works. This creates robotic delivery without the conviction that makes sales natural.

**The Training Inconsistency:** Sporadic training sessions instead of daily skill development. Sales skills require constant reinforcement to maintain peak performance.

### Sales Process Mistakes

**The Qualification Skip:** Jumping into presentations without confirming Budget, Authority, Need, and Timing. This leads to lengthy sales processes that end in "I need to think about it."

**The Frame Loss:** Not maintaining the professional frame established during setting and scheduling. Every interaction should reinforce that you're a legitimate, professional business.

**The Convenience Ignorance:** Making prospects conform to your schedule instead of being available when they want to buy. Convenience often trumps quality in purchase decisions.

**The Follow-up Failure:** Relying only on automated sequences without personal touch. Manual follow-ups at key moments dramatically increase show-up and close rates.

**The Testimonial Neglect:** Not regularly sharing customer success stories with your sales team. Conviction comes from seeing real results, not just drilling scripts.

### Management Mistakes

**The Skill Over System:** Focusing on individual performance improvements instead of systematic operational improvements. A 4x improvement from faster response beats a 20% improvement from better closing skills.

**The Recording Resistance:** Not recording all customer interactions due to technical concerns or privacy fears. You can't improve what you can't measure and review.

**The Separation Syndrome:** Keeping sales and customer success teams completely separate. This creates expectation mismatches and customer experience problems.

**The Activity Ignorance:** Not having clear protocols for what salespeople do when they're not on calls. Unstructured time leads to low productivity and missed opportunities.

**The Metric Myopia:** Only tracking close rates instead of the full funnel including response times, show-up rates, and appointment pull-forward success.
