## 2. FREQUENCY BEST PRACTICES

### How Many Emails Per Day?

**Maximum Tested**: Up to 6 emails per day

**Typical Schedule**: Every 2 hours from 9am-9pm

**Counter-Intuitive Truth**: Open rates did NOT diminish with high frequency when emails are value-dense.

### Finding Your Limit

```
PROCESS:
1. Start with 3 emails/day
2. Monitor open rates for 1-2 weeks
3. Add 4th email
4. If open rates hold â†’ Add 5th
5. If open rates drop â†’ Stay at previous level
6. Maximum tested: 6/day
```

| Traditional | <20% | 20-30% | 30-40% | 40-50% |
| Value-Dense | <40% | 40-50% | 50-60% | 60-70% |

### The Floor/Ceiling Concept

Your emails will have a natural range:
- Email 1: 70% open rate
- Email 2: 65% open rate
- Email 3: 55% open rate
- Email 4: 50% open rate

**This is normal.** Only pull back when you break the FLOOR significantly (e.g., suddenly 30% when you were consistently 50%+).

## 3. TIMING BY FUNNEL TYPE

### Call Funnel (48-72 Hour Window)

**Strategy**: Maximum frequency immediately

```
Day of Booking: 2-3 emails
Day 1: 4-6 emails
Day 2: 4-6 emails
Day of Call: 2-3 emails (morning)
```

### Webinar Funnel (1-2 Week Promotion)

**Strategy**: Light at start, heavy at end

```
Days 14-10: 1-2 emails total
Days 9-5: 1 email every 2 days
Days 4-2: 2-3 emails per day
Day 1: 4-6 emails
Day of Webinar: 3-4 emails (countdown)
```

**Why**: Hammer Them content remarketing handles the gap. Emails are most effective close to the event.

### Challenge Funnel (Multi-Day)

**Strategy**: Ramp up daily

```
Day 1: 3 emails
Day 2: 4 emails
Day 3: 5-6 emails (if showing works)
```

## 4. WHAT TO WRITE ABOUT

### Source Questions From:

**1. Sales Team**
```
Ask: "What questions are you annoyed having to repeatedly answer?"
Ask: "What do people show up asking that they should know beforehand?"
Ask: "What makes someone buy faster?"
Ask: "What prevents one-call closes?"
```

**2. Categories to Cover**

| Category | Purpose | Example Topics |
|----------|---------|----------------|
| Why NOT to buy | Remove fear | Refund process, what if it fails, worst case |
| Why TO buy | Create conviction | Success stories, ROI, transformation |
| Why buy NOW | Create urgency | Timing, market changes, availability |
| How it works | Reduce confusion | Process, timeline, what to expect |
| Common questions | Proactive education | Price, time commitment, requirements |
| Objection handling | Remove barriers | "I don't have time," "It's expensive" |

### The AI Workflow for Content

```
1. Record breakout video (5-10 min) answering one question
2. Transcribe the video
3. Feed to AI with sample emails in your voice
4. Prompt: "Turn this into a value-dense email that:
   - Answers the question directly in the email
   - Is 400-800 words
   - Includes a link to the video at the bottom
   - Maintains my voice and style"
5. Review, edit, deploy
```

## 5. EMAIL STRUCTURE

### The Value-Dense Email Formula

```
SUBJECT: [Specific question or insight hook]

Hey [Name],

[Hook that connects to their situation - 1-2 sentences]

Here's the short answer to [question]: [Direct answer]

The longer answer involves [expand with detail]:

[Point 1 with explanation]

[Point 2 with explanation]

[Point 3 with explanation]

[Bridge to next step - call, webinar, etc.]

Talk soon,
[Name]

P.S. If you want to go deeper, here's a [X]-minute video on this: [link]
```

### Bad vs Good Example

**BAD (Surface-Level Reminder)**:
```
Subject: Don't forget about tomorrow!

Hey,

Just a quick reminder about your call tomorrow at 2pm.

Here's the link: [link]

See you there!
```

**GOOD (Value-Dense)**:
```
Subject: The #1 thing holding back businesses like yours

Hey [Name],

Tomorrow at 2pm we'll dig into your [specific challenge].

Before we talk, I want to address something that comes up in 
almost every conversation I have:

"Why haven't my past attempts at [solution] worked?"

Here's what I've noticed after working with 40+ businesses:

1. They were optimizing the wrong metric
Most focus on [common mistake]. What actually moves the needle 
is [correct approach]. Here's why...

2. They didn't give it enough runway
[Explanation of realistic timeline and why]

3. They tried to do it all themselves
[Explanation of what requires expertise]

On our call, I'll dig into which of these might be 
affecting your situation specifically.

Come prepared to share:
- Your current [metric]
- What you've tried before
- Your timeline for [goal]

Talk soon,
[Name]

P.S. Here's a 7-minute breakdown of these three factors: [link]
```

## 6. THE CLICK-THROUGH REALITY

### Why Emails > Links

**Click-through rates**: 2-5% typical

**Translation**: 95-98% of people never click your links

**Implication**: If all your value is BEHIND the link, 95% of people never get it.

### The Solution

Put the value IN the email. Links are a bonus for the 2-5% who want to go deeper.

**Instead of**: "Click here to watch the video about X"

**Do this**: Explain X in the email. Add "Want to go deeper? Here's the video" at the bottom.

## 8. COMPLIANCE NOTES

### Key Regulations

- **CAN-SPAM**: Include unsubscribe, physical address
- **TCPA/California**: Texting restrictions by time
- **Texas**: Recent email/SMS restrictions
- **GDPR (EU)**: Consent and timing requirements

### Best Practice

Even at 6/day, you're fine IF:
- Content is genuinely valuable
- Clear unsubscribe option
- Respecting time-of-day regulations
- Not sending to people who didn't opt in

## 9. MEASURING SUCCESS

### KPIs to Track

| Metric | Target | What It Tells You |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| Open Rate | 50-70% | Subject line quality + list health |
| Click Rate | 2-5% | Secondary (most value in email) |
| Show Rate Lift | 10-20%+ | Emails are working |
| Sales Cycle | Shorter | Better pre-education |
| Close Rate | Higher | Better qualified, better framed |
| PIF Rate | Higher | More conviction |

### A/B Testing Priority

1. **Subject lines** (biggest impact on opens)
2. **Email length** (test 400 vs 800 words)
3. **Content topics** (which questions matter most)
4. **Frequency** (find your ceiling)
5. **Timing** (when in the day/sequence)
