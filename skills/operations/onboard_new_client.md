## Pre-Onboarding Preparation

### Why Onboarding is Critical

**The Data:**
- Proper onboarding reduces churn by 30-47%
- First 30 days determine if client stays or leaves
- 70% of clients who see results in first 30 days stay 12+ months
- Poor onboarding is the #1 reason for early churn

**Core Principle:**
The onboarding process is where you deliver on the promise you made during the sales call. This is where trust is built or broken.

### Pre-Onboarding Checklist

**Before Client Signs Contract:**
- [ ] Confirm service delivery capabilities
- [ ] Verify team capacity to take on new client
- [ ] Check all systems are operational
- [ ] Ensure necessary software/tools are available
- [ ] Confirm timeline expectations are realistic

**Immediately After Contract Signing:**
- [ ] Send welcome email within 1 hour
- [ ] Schedule onboarding call within 24-48 hours
- [ ] Send onboarding form link
- [ ] Add client to CRM with "New Client" tag
- [ ] Create client folder in project management system
- [ ] Set up internal client communication channel
- [ ] Assign account manager/CSM if applicable
- [ ] Prepare personalized onboarding materials

### Form Best Practices

**Design Principles:**
- Use conditional logic to show/hide questions
- Keep form to 15-25 questions maximum
- Use multiple choice when possible to speed completion
- Include "Other" options with text boxes
- Make critical questions required, others optional
- Use section breaks to organize information
- Include progress bar if form is long

**Tools to Use:**
- Typeform (best for experience)
- Google Forms (free, functional)
- Jotform (good middle ground)
- Airtable Forms (if using Airtable)
- Notion Forms (if using Notion)

**Timing:**
- Send form immediately after contract signing
- Set deadline of 24-48 hours before onboarding call
- Send reminder 24 hours before deadline
- Review responses before onboarding call
- Prepare questions based on their answers

## Onboarding Call Structure

### Pre-Call Preparation (15-30 minutes)

**Review Client Information:**
- Read contract and proposal thoroughly
- Review onboarding form responses completely
- Note any red flags or concerns
- Prepare specific questions based on their responses
- Research their business, competitors, industry
- Prepare relevant examples or case studies

**Prepare Materials:**
- Screen share presentation or document
- Welcome deck (if using one)
- Access to their accounts (if pre-granted)
- Process documentation to share
- Next steps checklist
- Calendar link for follow-up meetings

**Set Internal Context:**
- Brief team members who will work on account
- Set up internal communication channels
- Create project in project management system
- Set up time tracking (if applicable)
- Prepare internal kickoff notes

### Onboarding Call Agenda (60 minutes)

#### Phase 1: Welcome and Rapport (5 minutes)

**Opening Script:**
```
"Hi [Name], great to see you! Thanks for taking the time today. I know you're busy, so I want to make sure this hour is valuable for you.

Quick housekeeping: We have an hour together. In that time, we're going to:
1. Make sure we're crystal clear on your goals
2. Walk through exactly how we'll work together
3. Map out your first 30 days
4. Get you set up to see results quickly
5. Answer any questions you have

Sound good? Great. Before we dive in, tell me - what's got you most excited about working together?"
```

**Goals:**
- Build rapport and trust
- Set clear expectations for the call
- Get client engaged and talking
- Transition smoothly to discovery

#### Phase 2: Goals Clarification (10 minutes)

**Key Questions to Ask:**

```
1. "You mentioned in your form that your #1 goal is [goal]. Tell me more about that - what's driving this goal?"

2. "If we're talking 90 days from now and you're thrilled with our progress, what happened? What results did you see?"

3. "What does success look like in specific numbers? For example, if your goal is more leads, how many leads would make you say 'this is working'?"

4. "What's the why behind this goal? Why does this matter to your business right now?"

5. "Are there any constraints or challenges we should be aware of that might impact achieving this goal?"

6. "Who else in your organization cares about this goal, and what do they need to see?"
```

**Framework: Present vs. Future State**

Use this table structure during the call:

| Aspect | Current State | Desired State (30 days) | Desired State (90 days) |
|--------|---------------|-------------------------|-------------------------|
| [Metric 1] | [Current] | [30-day goal] | [90-day goal] |
| [Metric 2] | [Current] | [30-day goal] | [90-day goal] |
| [Process] | [Current situation] | [Improved process] | [Optimized process] |
| [Resource] | [Current resources] | [Additional resources] | [Full resources] |

**Deliverable:**
By end of this phase, you should have crystal clear, measurable goals with specific timelines.

#### Phase 3: Process Walkthrough (15 minutes)

**Service Delivery Overview:**

"Let me walk you through exactly how we'll work together to achieve these goals..."

**What to Cover:**

1. **The Overall Process**
   - High-level methodology
   - Key phases/stages
   - Typical timeline for each phase
   - What happens at each stage

2. **Your Responsibilities**
   - What we need from client
   - Timeline for providing materials
   - Access requirements
   - Approval processes
   - Response time expectations

3. **Our Responsibilities**
   - What we'll deliver
   - When we'll deliver it
   - Quality standards
   - How we'll communicate progress
   - What to do if issues arise

4. **Communication Cadence**
   - Regular check-in schedule
   - Reporting frequency
   - How to reach us
   - Emergency procedures
   - Feedback process

**Visual Aid Structure:**

Create a visual timeline showing:
```
Week 1:
- [Key activities]
- [Deliverables]
- [Client actions needed]

Week 2:
- [Key activities]
- [Deliverables]
- [Client actions needed]

Week 3-4:
- [Key activities]
- [Deliverables]
- [Milestones]

First Results Expected: [Timeframe]
```

#### Phase 5: Technical Setup (10 minutes)

**Account Access:**
- Walk through each platform needed
- Confirm access levels
- Test access in real-time if possible
- Document any access issues
- Set deadline for resolving access issues

**Asset Collection:**
- Review what assets are needed
- Confirm what client has ready
- Identify what needs to be created
- Set deadlines for asset delivery
- Discuss alternatives if assets aren't ready

**Tools and Systems:**
- Introduction to communication tools (Slack, etc.)
- How to access project management
- How to view progress/reports
- How to submit requests or feedback
- Emergency contact procedures

#### Phase 6: Q&A and Concerns (10 minutes)

**Proactive Concern Addressing:**

"Before we wrap up, I want to address some common questions clients have..."

**Common Concerns to Address:**

1. **Timeline Concerns:**
   "I know you're eager to see results. Here's the realistic timeline..."

2. **Resource Concerns:**
   "I know you're busy. Here's exactly what time commitment we need from you..."

3. **Technical Concerns:**
   "If you're worried about the technical side, don't be. Here's how we handle that..."

4. **Results Concerns:**
   "What if results don't happen as quickly as expected? Here's our process..."

5. **Communication Concerns:**
   "Worried about being left in the dark? Here's our communication guarantee..."

**Open Q&A:**
- "What questions do you have?"
- "What concerns should we address?"
- "What are you worried about?"
- "What would make you feel more confident?"

   - Clearly define scope and exclusions
   - Set realistic expectations

2. **Review Phase:**
   - Allow client time to review (at least 24-48 hours)
   - Offer to discuss any concerns
   - Be flexible on reasonable requests
   - Document all agreed changes
   - Have final version reviewed

3. **Signature Phase:**
   - Use electronic signature platform (DocuSign, HelloSign, PandaDoc)
   - Require signatures from authorized parties
   - Ensure all pages are initialed/signed as required
   - Provide fully executed copy immediately
   - Store in secure location

4. **Active Phase:**
   - Review contract before major decisions
   - Ensure compliance with all terms
   - Track deliverable deadlines
   - Document any variations or changes
   - Use for scope management

5. **Renewal/Termination Phase:**
   - Set reminders 60-90 days before renewal
   - Review performance against contract terms
   - Negotiate renewal terms if continuing
   - Follow termination procedures if ending
   - Ensure all obligations are met

## Retention-Focused Onboarding

### The Retention Mindset

**Core Principle:**
Client retention starts from day one. Everything in onboarding should be designed to:
1. Demonstrate value quickly
2. Build trust consistently
3. Set realistic expectations
4. Create positive momentum
5. Establish strong relationship

### Red Flags to Watch For

**During Onboarding Process:**

ðŸš© **Client doesn't complete onboarding form**
- Action: Personal outreach to understand why
- Follow-up: Offer to complete over phone call
- Escalation: May indicate lack of commitment

ðŸš© **Client misses or reschedules onboarding call multiple times**
- Action: Gauge actual level of interest
- Consider: May not be right time for them
- Option: Offer to pause start date

ðŸš© **Client has unclear or unrealistic goals**
- Action: Invest extra time in goals clarification
- Required: Reset expectations before proceeding
- Document: Ensure agreement on realistic outcomes

ðŸš© **Client is unresponsive to requests for access/materials**
- Action: Make it as easy as possible
- Provide: Step-by-step instructions
- Offer: To do it for them (if appropriate)
- Timeline: Set firm deadline and consequences

ðŸš© **Client questions process or methodology repeatedly**
- Action: More thorough explanation needed
- Consider: May not be right fit
- Address: Underlying concerns about investment

ðŸš© **Client mentions previous bad experiences**
- Action: Understand what went wrong
- Reassure: Explain how you're different
- Document: Their concerns to avoid same issues

**During First 30 Days:**

ðŸš© **Client is difficult to reach or unresponsive**
- Action: Try multiple communication channels
- Discuss: Their preferred communication style
- Consider: May not have time to be good client

ðŸš© **Client doesn't review or approve deliverables timely**
- Action: Simplify approval process
- Make easier: Provide specific yes/no questions
- Set: Clear deadlines with consequences

ðŸš© **Client requests frequent scope changes**
- Action: Document all changes clearly
- Discuss: Impact on timeline and costs
- May need: Scope change agreement

ðŸš© **Client focuses only on negatives, ignores wins**
- Action: Proactively highlight wins
- Discuss: Perspective and expectations
- Consider: May never be satisfied

ðŸš© **Client doesn't implement recommendations**
- Action: Understand barriers to implementation
- Offer: More hands-on support
- Discuss: Whether continued engagement makes sense
