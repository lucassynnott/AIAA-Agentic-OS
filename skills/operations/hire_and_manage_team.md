## The ROI of Proper Hiring

### The Data on CSM Hiring

**Critical Statistics:**
- 73% of CSM hires fail within the first 18 months
- Teams without systematic management achieve 30% lower retention rates
- Teams with structured oversight have 47% lower turnover
- CSMs can generate six figures per year in ROI when hired correctly
- Poor onboarding is the #1 reason for team member failure

**The Founder's Dilemma:**
Most founders hire someone to "remove themselves from client-facing work" rather than hiring someone who will actually drive client results. This leads to hiring an overpaid customer support rep instead of a true Customer Success Manager.

### Core Principle: Experience Matching

**The Ideal Hire Has BOTH:**
1. Client Success Management experience (CSM framework and skills)
2. Business/industry knowledge (understands your niche/service)

**If You Must Choose One:**
- **CSM Experience > Business Knowledge**: Faster ramp-up (2-4 weeks)
  - Easier to teach business specifics to someone who understands the CSM role
  - They already know frameworks, communication strategies, retention tactics

- **Business Knowledge Only**: Much longer training period
  - Only viable if they have 1-2+ years solid experience in your exact industry
  - Will require extensive training on CSM fundamentals
  - Higher risk of failure

**Never Hire:**
- Someone with neither CSM experience nor business knowledge
- This is a recipe for disaster and negative ROI

### The Difference: CSM vs Account Manager

**Customer Success Manager:**
- Ensures client adoption of product/service
- Drives client engagement and ROI
- Proactive relationship management
- Strategic problem-solving
- Handles multiple business types/niches
- Minimal done-for-you work
- Requires sales skills for renewals and expansion

**Account Manager:**
- Heavy fulfillment focus
- Done-for-you service delivery
- Same niche/service repeatedly
- More tactical, less strategic
- Typically e-commerce or similar

## Hiring Framework for CSMs

### Pre-Interview Requirements

**Minimum Qualifications:**
- [ ] 1-2+ years CSM or client success experience (minimum)
- [ ] Demonstrated metrics of client retention or expansion
- [ ] Experience in similar agency model or industry
- [ ] Proven sales experience (massive plus)
- [ ] Technical proficiency with SaaS platforms and CRM systems
- [ ] Strong communication skills (written and verbal)

**Red Flags to Avoid:**
- Generic statements like "I have people skills" without specifics
- No metrics or measurable outcomes from previous roles
- Customer service experience claimed as CSM experience (NOT the same)
- Job hopping with no clear progression
- Inability to articulate specific CSM frameworks or strategies

### Hiring Funnel Structure

**Use a Trello Board/CRM Approach:**

Treat hiring like a sales funnel. Create columns for:

1. **Resources Tab**
   - Link to job application (Google Form or Typeform)
   - Link to assessment/homework
   - Link to screening interview calendar
   - Link to final interview calendar

2. **New Applications**
   - Auto-populate from application form (use Zapier)
   - Review application quality
   - Check for red flags

3. **Screening Interview Scheduled**
   - First call to assess basic fit
   - 15-30 minutes with hiring manager
   - Verify experience and communication skills

4. **Assessment/Homework Assigned**
   - Only for candidates who pass screening
   - Real-world scenario-based homework
   - Evaluates problem-solving and approach

5. **Culture Fit Interview**
   - Interview with existing team member
   - 15-minute peer evaluation
   - Get secondary perspective

6. **Final Interview**
   - Decision-maker interview (founder/head of CS)
   - Final vetting and offer discussion
   - 30-60 minutes

7. **Hired** or **Disqualified**

### Job Application Structure

**Application Form (Google Form/Typeform):**

**Section 1: Introduction and Role Description**
- Company overview and mission
- Role responsibilities and expectations
- OTE (On-Target Earnings) and compensation structure
- Why they should be excited about this opportunity

**Section 2: Basic Information**
- Full name
- Email address
- Phone number
- Location/timezone
- How they heard about the opportunity

**Section 3: Experience Questions**
- What do you know about our company?
- What specifically stuck out about this role?
- Why do you think you're a good fit?
- What is your experience with [relevant service/industry]?
- What are your career goals (monetary and non-monetary)?
- How does this role play into your long-term vision?

**Section 4: Availability**
- Are you available for full-time commitment?
- If offered the position, how quickly can you start?

**Section 5: Initial Assessment**
"To complete your application, please complete this quick assignment:"

**Example Assignment:**
"You're onboarding a new client for [your service]. Create a welcome message (300-500 words) that guides them on the best next steps to get started. Include what they should do in the first 7 days to see quick wins."

**BONUS (but required):** Record a Loom video (3-5 minutes) walking through your welcome message.

**Why This Works:** This filters for people willing to go above and beyond. Those who skip the Loom are not A-players.

## Interview Process and Structure

### 60-Minute Interview Framework

**Minutes 1-15: Business Knowledge Assessment**

Evaluate their understanding of:
- Your business model
- Your industry and niche
- Competitive landscape
- Your specific challenges

**Key Questions:**
- "What do you know about our business?"
- "Who do you think our ideal client profile is?"
- "What challenges do you think we face with this type of client?"
- "Have you ever used our service or similar ones?"

**What You're Looking For:**
- Specific research (not just generic industry knowledge)
- Mentioned your services, pricing, case studies
- Looked up Trust Pilot reviews, client testimonials
- Asked intelligent questions about your business

**Red Flags:**
- Generic answers ("You help businesses scale")
- No research done
- Can't articulate what makes you different

**Minutes 16-30: Scenario-Based Problem Solving**

Present 2-3 real-world client situations. Assess:
- Problem-solving approach
- Communication strategy
- Balance between client needs and business needs
- Strategic thinking vs tactical reaction

**Example Scenarios:**

**Scenario 1: Disengaged Client**
"A client you've been working with for 3 months has stopped responding to emails and hasn't shown up to the last two scheduled calls. They're paying $5K/month. How do you handle this?"

**Scenario 2: Unrealistic Expectations**
"A new client expects results in 2 weeks when your typical timeline is 4-6 weeks. They're getting frustrated. How do you manage this situation?"

**Scenario 3: Client Wants Refund**
"A client has been with you for 6 weeks, followed 30% of your recommendations, and now says 'this isn't working' and wants a refund. Walk me through your response."

**Scenario 4: Coaching Method Criticism**
"A client has communicated privately about their disappointment with your coaching methods. They're hinting at wanting to switch to a new CSM or receive a refund. How do you respond?"

**What You're Evaluating:**
- Do they ask clarifying questions?
- Do they take a strategic or emotional approach?
- Do they understand client psychology?
- Can they balance empathy with business requirements?
- Do they demonstrate problem-solving frameworks?

**Minutes 31-50: Experience Deep Dive**

Probe specific examples from their past work:
- "Tell me about a time you prevented a client from churning"
- "Walk me through your most successful client retention strategy"
- "How have you handled expansion/upsells in previous roles?"
- "Describe a conflict you had with a client and how you resolved it"
- "What's your approach to managing 15-20 active clients?"

**Look For:**
- Specific stories with measurable outcomes
- Use of frameworks and systems (not just "I talked to them")
- Data and metrics ("increased retention by 25%")
- Self-awareness about what worked and what didn't

**Minutes 51-60: Culture Fit and Alignment**

Assess:
- Will they blend well with your team?
- Do they share your company values?
- Are they coachable and growth-minded?
- Do they have long-term thinking?

**Questions:**
- "Tell me about the best manager you've ever had"
- "What type of work environment do you thrive in?"
- "How do you handle feedback and criticism?"
- "Where do you see yourself in 2-3 years?"

**Remember:** CSMs talk to everyone (sales, fulfillment, founders). They need to be cultural fits across the entire organization.

## Assessment and Vetting

### Homework Assignment Structure

**Purpose:** Evaluate real-world skills in a practical scenario

**Example CSM Assessment:**

**Instructions:**
"You're now the Customer Success Manager for [Company Name]. Below are 4 scenarios you might encounter. For each scenario, write out your response (200-300 words each)."

**Scenario 1: Onboarding**
"A new client just signed up for [service]. Write the email you would send them after their onboarding call to summarize next steps and keep them engaged."

**Scenario 2: Check-In Call**
"You're doing a 30-day check-in call with a client. They've implemented 50% of your recommendations but haven't seen major results yet. How do you structure this call?"

**Scenario 3: Re-Engagement**
"A client has gone dark for 2 weeks. Write the outreach sequence (2-3 messages) you would use to re-engage them."

**Scenario 4: Objection Handling**
"A client says: 'I don't have time for this right now, can we pause our engagement for a month?' How do you respond?"

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Communication quality (clear, professional, empathetic)
- Problem-solving approach (strategic vs reactive)
- Understanding of client psychology
- Ability to balance business needs with client needs
- Use of frameworks or systems

**Passing Threshold:**
Only advance candidates who score 7+/10 on:
- Communication clarity
- Strategic thinking
- Client-first mindset
- Professional tone

## The 23-Day CSM Onboarding Framework

### Foundation Principle

**Master Onboarding Process FIRST Before Touching Clients:**
- CSMs must thoroughly understand your onboarding process before engaging with actual customers
- This isn't preference - it's backed by data
- Every failed CSM started with inadequate onboarding knowledge
- If they don't know how to onboard a client properly, the client will churn AND your brand reputation will suffer

**Training Order Principle:**
Always train in the order of work they'll be doing:
1. Onboarding process (first thing they'll do)
2. Client meetings and ongoing management (second)
3. Renewals and backend sales (later)

### Pre-Onboarding Requirements

**Before Day 1, Have Ready:**

**1. Comprehensive Documentation**
- Business operations manual
- Client workflows and processes
- Service delivery methodology
- Company history, mission, values
- Target market and positioning
- All SOPs in written and video format

**2. Knowledge Verification System**
- Quizzes and assessments to test comprehension
- Google Forms for knowledge checks
- Scoring system (must pass with 80%+ to advance)

**3. Video Learning Library**
- Loom videos of actual onboarding calls
- Client case study interviews
- Service delivery walkthroughs
- Tool navigation tutorials (all tech stack)
- Company overview from founder

**4. Tech Stack Setup**
- Calendar system (Calendly) configured
- Slack/communication access
- CRM access and training
- Project management tools
- Email templates and signature
- Fathom or call recording setup
- All necessary software licenses

**Critical:** Don't just give them business/service SOPs. Include SOPs for:
- How to manage their calendar
- How to set up meetings
- Email communication standards
- Cross-team communication protocols
- Client communication best practices

### Days 1-7: Foundational Learning

**Daily Structure:**
- 30-60 minute daily 1-on-1 with manager (morning or end of day)
- Review yesterday's learning
- Answer questions from self-study
- Set goals for today
- Knowledge checks and comprehension tests

**Focus Areas:**

**Day 1-2: Business 101**
- Company history and mission
- Target market and ideal client profile
- Competitive positioning
- Service offerings and packages
- Customer contract length and lifecycle
- Timeline to first win (what's realistic?)
- Case studies and success stories

**Day 3-4: Service Offerings Deep Dive**
- All packages and pricing
- Deal sizes and contract terms
- Success metrics and KPIs
- Timeline to results (30/60/90 days)
- What makes a successful client
- What causes client churn

**Day 5-7: Tech Stack and Tools**
- CRM navigation and workflows
- Project management software
- Communication tools (Slack, email)
- Calendar and scheduling systems
- Call recording and note-taking
- Workflow automations and integrations

**Assessment:** Quiz on business knowledge, service offerings, and tech stack
- Must score 7+/10 to proceed
- If below 7, additional training required

### Days 8-15: Mock Calls and Shadowing

**Onboarding Call Mastery:**

**Watch 3-5 Full Onboarding Call Recordings (TWICE each)**
- First watch: Take notes on structure and flow
- Second watch: Map out questions and reasoning

**What to Analyze:**
- Why did the CSM ask that specific question?
- How did they handle objections?
- How did they set expectations?
- What frameworks were used?
- How did they create quick wins?
- What next steps were assigned?

**Live Call Breakdown Sessions:**
Schedule 2-3 sessions where you:
- Screen share a call recording
- Watch together and pause frequently
- Explain your thinking and strategy
- Answer their questions in real-time
- Record the breakdown session (becomes future training material)

**Role Play Exercises:**
- CSM acts as client, you conduct onboarding (show them how)
- You act as client, CSM conducts onboarding (evaluate their approach)
- Record and review together
- Repeat until they score 8+/10 on execution

**Shadow Real Calls:**
- Have new CSM listen to 5-10 live onboarding calls
- They observe but don't speak
- Take detailed notes
- Debrief after each call

**Assessment:** Mock onboarding call where you play difficult client
- Must handle 3-5 common objections smoothly
- Must follow onboarding framework
- Must set clear next steps
- Pass threshold: 8+/10

### Days 16-23: Guided Client Interaction

**Graduated Responsibility:**

**Days 16-18: Assisted Onboarding**
- Conduct real onboarding calls WITH supervision
- Manager on call but doesn't speak unless necessary
- New CSM leads the call
- Immediate feedback after each call

**Days 19-21: Independent Onboarding with Review**
- Conduct onboarding calls independently
- Manager reviews recording within 24 hours
- Detailed feedback provided
- Adjust and improve

**Days 22-23: Full Independence**
- Manage onboarding calls without supervision
- Begin taking on client management responsibilities
- Scheduled review of first 10 client interactions

**Quality Checks:**
- Review all call recordings
- Check documentation completion
- Verify client satisfaction (quick survey)
- Assess confidence level (self-rating + manager rating)

### Ongoing Assessment

**Daily Confidence Checks:**
"On a scale of 1-10, how confident do you feel with [today's material]?"
- Score below 7 = additional focus needed before proceeding

**Weekly Knowledge Verification:**
- Quiz on previous week's learning
- Must maintain 80%+ scores
- Gaps identified and addressed immediately

**30-Day Review:**
- Comprehensive performance evaluation
- Client feedback review
- KPI assessment (retention, engagement, satisfaction)
- Decision point: continue, additional training, or terminate

## Weekly Management Rhythm

### The Four Pillars of CSM Management

**Monday: Power Hour (60 minutes)**
- **Time:** 9:00 AM (first thing Monday morning)
- **Who:** All CSMs + Manager
- **Format:** Silent work session on high-leverage tasks
- **Purpose:** Get critical work done as a team

**Tasks Include:**
- Churn reactivation outreach
- Trust Pilot collection
- Client check-ins
- Health score sheet updates
- Strategic planning for at-risk clients

**Why Morning:**
- Clients rarely book 9 AM calls
- Sets productive tone for the week
- Accountability through group participation
- Manager presence ensures work gets done

**Tuesday: One-on-One (30-60 minutes)**
- **Time:** Consistent day/time each week
- **Who:** Manager + Individual CSM
- **Format:** Performance development conversation

**Agenda:**
- Review last week's performance and metrics
- Discuss challenges and roadblocks
- Review 1-2 call recordings together
- Goal setting for current week
- Coaching and skill development
- Answer questions

**New Hires:** Extend to 60 minutes for first 90 days for additional training time

**Wednesday: Team Training (60-90 minutes)**
- **Time:** 9:00 AM
- **Who:** All CSMs + Manager + Relevant team members
- **Purpose:** Skills development and knowledge expansion

**Training Topics Rotate:**
- New service offerings or packages
- Updated methodologies or frameworks
- Industry trends and insights
- Advanced communication strategies
- Sales and renewal tactics
- Case study analysis
- Competitive intelligence
- Product/service updates

**Why Weekly Training Matters:**
- Business is always evolving
- CSMs need continuous learning
- Ensures alignment across team
- Prevents knowledge gaps
- Builds team cohesion

**Friday: Leadership Alignment (30-60 minutes)**
- **Time:** End of week
- **Who:** Founder + CSM Manager + All CSMs
- **Purpose:** Company updates and team synchronization

**Agenda:**
1. **Founder Updates (10-15 min)**
   - Company progress and wins
   - Strategic direction updates
   - New initiatives or changes
   - Transitions (what we're moving toward/away from)

2. **Round Robin Check-Ins (20-30 min)**
   - Each CSM shares:
     - Weekly wins (case studies, testimonials, referrals)
     - What's working for clients
     - What's NOT working for clients
     - Challenges or roadblocks

3. **Open Discussion (10-15 min)**
   - Identify patterns across multiple CSMs
   - Address systemic issues
   - Celebrate team wins
   - Preview next week

**Why Founder Presence Matters:**
- Higher accountability
- Team takes meetings more seriously
- Direct communication from leadership
- CSMs feel connected to company vision
- Prevents information silos

### Morning Focus Strategy

**Why Schedule Key Meetings Before 10 AM:**
- Clients rarely book calls before 10 AM
- Opens up CSM calendar for client meetings later
- High energy and focus in the morning
- Critical work gets done before distractions
- Creates consistent rhythm and structure

**Typical Weekly Calendar:**
- Monday 9 AM: Power Hour
- Tuesday 9 AM: One-on-One
- Wednesday 9 AM: Team Training
- Friday 4 PM: Leadership Alignment
- Rest of time: Client-facing work

## Performance Management and Quality Control

### Call Quality Management System

**Purpose:** Continuous quality monitoring, not reactive firefighting

**Random Call Reviews:**
- Review 2-3 calls per CSM per week
- Don't announce which calls - element of surprise
- Creates expectation: "Every call could be reviewed"
- CSMs perform at higher level consistently

**What to Evaluate:**

**1. Communication Quality (Weight: 35%)**
- Ability to translate complex concepts into friendly language
- Active listening and empathy
- Clarity of explanations
- Professional but personable tone
- Pacing and engagement

**2. Problem Solving (Weight: 30%)**
- Diagnostic thinking (identifying root cause)
- Solution creativity (multiple options presented)
- Resource utilization (leveraging available tools)
- Effectiveness in addressing challenge
- Follow-through on commitments

**3. Relationship Management (Weight: 25%)**
- Trust building (did client open up?)
- Rapport establishment
- Client satisfaction (did they leave happy?)
- Long-term relationship development
- Value demonstration

**4. Process Adherence (Weight: 10%)**
- Documentation complete and accurate
- Notes taken during call
- Action items clearly assigned
- Follow-up scheduled
- CRM updated properly
- Next steps communicated to client

**Scoring System:**
- 9-10: Exceptional - use as training example
- 7-8: Good - minor improvements needed
- 5-6: Acceptable - requires coaching
- Below 5: Unacceptable - immediate intervention

**Feedback Delivery:**
- Send Fathom recording link
- Provide written feedback (specific timestamps)
- Voice note walking through highlights and improvements
- Schedule review call if score below 7

**Frequency by Experience:**
- New hires (first 90 days): Review every call
- 90 days - 1 year: Review 3-4 calls per week
- 1+ years: Review 2 calls per week

### Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

**Primary Metrics:**

**Client-Facing KPIs:**
- Client retention rate (goal: 85%+)
- Client satisfaction scores (goal: 8+/10 average)
- Time to first win (goal: under 30 days)
- Client engagement rate (attending calls, completing tasks)
- Renewal rate (goal: 90%+)
- Expansion revenue (upsells and additional services)

**Activity KPIs:**
- Calls per week (high-touch: 15-25, mid-touch: 10-15, low-touch: 5-10)
- Response time to client messages (goal: under 4 hours)
- Proactive check-ins completed
- Documentation quality score
- Client health score maintenance

**Quality KPIs:**
- Call quality average score (goal: 8+/10)
- Client testimonial collection (goal: 1 per month per CSM)
- Referral generation (track referrals sourced)
- Case study participation (clients willing to be featured)

### CSM Capacity Planning

**High-Touch CSMs:**
- 15-25 calls per week
- 60-90 minute call duration
- Maximum 8-12 active clients
- Deep, intensive client relationships

**Mid-Touch CSMs:**
- 10-15 calls per week
- 20-30 minute call duration
- Maximum 15-20 active clients
- Balance of depth and breadth

**Low-Touch CSMs:**
- 5-10 calls per week
- 15-20 minute call duration
- Maximum 25-30 active clients
- Efficient, focused interactions

**Warning Signs of Overload:**
- Response time increasing
- Call quality scores dropping
- Missing scheduled meetings
- Documentation falling behind
- Client satisfaction decreasing

### Progressive Discipline Framework

**Performance Issues - Escalation Path:**

**Step 1: Coaching Conversation**
- Identify specific performance gap
- Discuss root cause
- Create improvement plan
- Set clear expectations and timeline
- Document conversation

**Step 2: Written Warning**
- Formal documentation of continued issues
- Review previous coaching
- Updated improvement plan with measurable goals
- 30-day review checkpoint
- Consequences clearly stated

**Step 3: Final Warning**
- Last opportunity to improve
- Daily check-ins with manager
- Weekly performance reviews
- 14-day improvement period
- Termination discussed as option

**Step 4: Termination**
- Performance has not improved
- Client experience being negatively impacted
- Team morale affected
- Clear documentation trail
- Handled professionally and respectfully

**Immediate Termination Situations:**
- Theft or dishonesty
- Violation of client confidentiality
- Harassment or discrimination
- Gross negligence causing major client loss
- Violation of company values

## Scaling Your Team Through People

### The Leverage Principle

**Core Philosophy:**
Your goal as founder is to hit your revenue targets without doing all the work yourself. This requires building systems where talented people can execute your vision.

**From Alex Hermozi's Framework:**

**Professionals vs Amateurs:**
- Amateurs focus on lead generation
- Professionals focus on customer acquisition (paying customers)

**Getting Rich Through Other People:**
- You have 24 hours in a day
- You can't scale beyond your own time constraints
- Hiring multiplies your output
- Systems allow people to produce without constant oversight

**Key Insight:**
If you can destroy 8-figure companies, you can build them too. Failure teaches faster than success when you analyze what went wrong.

### The Burnout Prevention Framework

**Warning Signs of Founder Burnout:**
- Working 80+ hours per week consistently
- Making all client decisions personally
- No vacation in 12+ months
- Revenue growth stalled despite more work
- Team constantly asking "what should I do?"
- Clients only want to talk to you
- Feeling trapped in your business

**The Solution: Systematic Delegation**

**Phase 1: Document Everything (Weeks 1-4)**
- Record every client interaction
- Create SOPs as you work
- Use Loom to capture your process
- Build knowledge base

**Phase 2: Hire and Train (Weeks 5-12)**
- Follow hiring framework from this guide
- Use 23-day onboarding system
- Shadow your work
- Gradual responsibility transfer

**Phase 3: Manage and Optimize (Weeks 13+)**
- Weekly management rhythm
- Quality control systems
- Continuous improvement
- Team training and development

**Phase 4: Scale and Lead (6+ months)**
- Hire manager to run CSM team
- Focus on strategy and growth
- Build additional revenue streams
- Work ON business, not IN business

### Building a Self-Managing Team

**Characteristics of Self-Managing Teams:**
- Clear SOPs for all recurring tasks
- Documented decision-making frameworks
- Regular training and development
- Strong communication culture
- Accountability systems in place
- Metrics-driven performance
- Peer coaching and collaboration

**Your Role Evolution:**
1. **Months 1-3:** Do everything, document everything
2. **Months 4-6:** Hire and train first CSM, heavy management
3. **Months 7-12:** Multiple CSMs, systematic management, reduced day-to-day
4. **Year 2+:** CSM Manager role, focus on strategy and growth

**Hiring Your First CSM Manager:**

**When You Need One:**
- You have 3+ CSMs
- You're spending 15+ hours per week on CSM management
- CSMs are constantly asking you questions
- You want to focus on business growth
- Your management is becoming a bottleneck

**Ideal Profile:**
- Has been a CSM for 2+ years
- Managed or trained other CSMs before
- Strong leadership and communication skills
- Detail-oriented with systems thinking
- Aligned with company values

**Transition Process:**
- Promote from within when possible (they know your systems)
- 90-day transition period with daily check-ins
- Transfer all management frameworks from this guide
- Gradual responsibility handoff
- Weekly strategy meetings (you + manager)

### The Ultimate Goal

**Your Business Should Run Without You:**
- CSM team managed by capable leader
- Sales team closing deals independently
- Fulfillment systematized and delegated
- Marketing on autopilot or managed by specialist
- Operations handled by capable COO/Ops Manager

**Your Role as Founder:**
- Strategic direction
- Key relationships and partnerships
- Product/service innovation
- Company culture and values
- High-level problem solving
- Investor/board relations (if applicable)

**Time Allocation Goal (Year 2+):**
- 20% strategy and vision
- 20% key relationships
- 20% innovation and R&D
- 20% team development and culture
- 20% personal development and rest

### Manager's Weekly Prep Checklist

**Monday Power Hour Prep:**
- [ ] Identify high-priority tasks for the week
- [ ] Prepare list of at-risk clients
- [ ] Review team capacity and workload

**Tuesday One-on-Ones Prep:**
- [ ] Review each CSM's performance metrics
- [ ] Select 1-2 call recordings to review together
- [ ] Prepare coaching points and feedback
- [ ] Update individual development plans

**Wednesday Team Training Prep:**
- [ ] Select training topic for the week
- [ ] Prepare materials (slides, videos, case studies)
- [ ] Send pre-work if needed
- [ ] Schedule any guest speakers

**Friday Leadership Alignment Prep:**
- [ ] Compile company updates
- [ ] Review team wins for the week
- [ ] Identify systemic issues to discuss
- [ ] Prepare agenda and talking points

**Ongoing:**
- [ ] Review 2-3 call recordings per CSM per week
- [ ] Respond to team questions within 4 hours
- [ ] Monitor client satisfaction scores
- [ ] Update team capacity planning

## Key Takeaways

1. **Hiring is a Sales Process:** Treat it systematically with a funnel, stages, and conversion metrics

2. **Experience Matters:** Prioritize CSM experience over business knowledge when you must choose

3. **Onboarding is Make-or-Break:** The 23-day framework prevents the 73% failure rate

4. **Management is Rhythm:** Weekly structure (Power Hour, 1-on-1s, Training, Team Call) creates consistency

5. **Quality Control is Continuous:** Review calls regularly, don't wait for problems

6. **Founder Involvement is Temporary:** Build systems to remove yourself from day-to-day

7. **People are Your Leverage:** You can't scale beyond your own time - hiring multiplies your impact

8. **Prevention > Firefighting:** Structured management prevents burnout and team turnover

9. **Culture Eats Strategy:** CSMs must fit your values and work well across the organization

10. **Document Everything:** SOPs and training materials compound over time and make future hires easier
