# SKILL BIBLE: Competitive Analysis & Research

> **Purpose**: Analyzing competitors and conducting market research.
> **Word Count Target**: 2,000+
> **Last Updated**: 2026-01-02

---

## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Know your competition. Understanding competitors helps you differentiate, price correctly, and find opportunities.

**Why Competitive Analysis Matters**:
- Identify differentiation opportunities
- Understand market positioning
- Learn from their successes/failures
- Inform your strategy

---

## 2. IDENTIFYING COMPETITORS

### Types of Competitors

**Direct**: Same service, same audience
**Indirect**: Different service, same problem
**Aspirational**: Where you want to be

### Finding Competitors

- Google search for your service
- Ask prospects who else they're considering
- Industry directories
- Social media searches
- Client feedback

---

## 3. WHAT TO ANALYZE

### Marketing & Positioning

- Website messaging
- Value proposition
- Target audience
- Unique differentiators
- Content strategy

### Offer & Pricing

- Service offerings
- Pricing (if visible)
- Guarantees
- Package structure

### Sales Process

- How they generate leads
- Sales funnel
- Demo/call process
- Follow-up

### Content & Authority

- Blog/content topics
- Social presence
- Thought leadership
- Case studies

### Reviews & Reputation

- Testimonials
- Review sites
- Social mentions
- Complaints

---

## 4. RESEARCH METHODS

### Public Sources

- Website analysis
- Social media
- Content/blog
- Pricing pages
- Case studies

### Deeper Research

- Sign up for their content
- Book a sales call (ethical considerations)
- Read reviews
- Talk to their clients
- Industry reports

### Tools

- SimilarWeb (traffic)
- BuiltWith (tech stack)
- SEMrush/Ahrefs (SEO/ads)
- Social listening tools

---

## 6. USING INSIGHTS

### Positioning

- Differentiate from competitors
- Own a unique space
- Address gaps they leave

### Messaging

- Speak to what competitors miss
- Handle objections about alternatives
- Position against them (subtly)

### Sales

- Know how to compare
- Address "why not them?"
- Use competitive intel in objection handling

---

## 7. ONGOING MONITORING

### What to Track

- Website/messaging changes
- New offerings
- Pricing changes
- Content themes
- Hiring (growth signals)

### Frequency

- Deep analysis: Quarterly
- Light monitoring: Monthly
- Alerts: Ongoing
