# SKILL BIBLE: Five Lead Gen Concepts for $10M Scale

> Source: Matthew Larsen (1000x Leads)
> Context: Five critical concepts required to scale any service business to $10M/year

---

## Executive Summary

Scaling to $10M requires understanding five interconnected concepts: complete funnels, the infinite cycle, more-better-new sequencing, results lag, and leverage. Missing any one of these prevents scaling past a ceiling.

---

## Concept 1: The Complete Lead Generation Funnel

### The Vehicle Analogy

> "If you just have every piece of the vehicle except the steering wheel, the brakes, and the door, that basically makes it useless. Same thing with a lead generation funnel."

### The Four Problems to Solve

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Not enough traffic | Paid ads, content, outreach, partnerships |
| Can't capture/nurture/qualify leads | Lead magnets, email flows, community |
| All clients from referrals (uncontrollable) | Cold traffic conversion at scale |
| Only 3% in-market | Offers for every level |

### Why You Need ALL Four Traffic Sources

**Reason 1: Things Change**
- Algorithms shift
- Accounts get banned
- Methods mature/sophisticate
- New methods emerge

> "If you have all your eggs in one basket and something changes, you are effectively going to go to zero."

**Reason 2: They Amplify Each Other**

Example flow:
1. They see your paid ad
2. Click your profile
3. Watch 3-4 reels/posts
4. View you more favorably
5. More likely to take action

Or:
1. They've consumed your content
2. Get outreach from you
3. Recognize your name
4. Much more likely to respond

### The 97% Profit Opportunity

> "Only 3% of people are in-market right now. We can double or triple revenue AND double profit by having different offers for different levels."

**Offer Stack for the 97%:**
- Digital products (under $100, ~95% margin)
- Affiliate links (100% margin)
- Referrals to cheaper providers (earn a fee)
- Courses (repackaged training, ~95% margin)
- Programs (course + coaching, ~95% margin)

## Concept 2: The Infinite Cycle of Lead Generation

### The Positive Cycle

```
More Leads
    ↓
Less Needy
    ↓
Easier to Sell
    ↓
Don't Take Nightmare Clients + More Clients
    ↓
More Referrals → More Leads
    ↓
Raise Prices (inverted supply/demand)
    ↓
Better Clients + More Margin
    ↓
Better Hires
    ↓
Less Time IN Business, More Time ON Business
    ↓
Reinvest in Growth
    ↓
More Leads
    ↓
[Cycle Repeats]
```

### The Negative Cycle (Death Spiral)

```
Less Leads
    ↓
More Needy
    ↓
Harder to Sell
    ↓
Must Take Nightmare Clients + Fewer Clients
    ↓
Fewer Referrals
    ↓
Lower Prices (no leverage)
    ↓
Worse Clients + Lower Margins
    ↓
Worse Hires (or can't hire)
    ↓
More Time Babysitting
    ↓
Less Time on Growth
    ↓
Less Leads
    ↓
[Cycle Repeats Down]
```

> "Everybody is either on the positive side of this cycle or the negative side. There's not really any in between."

## Concept 3: More → Better → New

### The Alex Hormozi Principle

**The Order:**
1. **MORE** - Do more of what's working until it stops working
2. **BETTER** - Make it better until you can't improve it more
3. **NEW** - Only THEN try something new

### Common Mistake

> "I have so many clients who say 'Oh this is working, let's just try something completely different.'"

### Examples

| Situation | Wrong Move | Right Move |
|-----------|-----------|------------|
| 5K cold emails/day, 2-3 calls | A/B test script | Send 50K/day |
| $1K/day ads, acceptable CPA | New ad angles | 5x the budget |
| 2 YouTube videos/week | Try TikTok | Do 7 videos/week |

**Why this works:**
- Less thought power required
- More leverage from one thing
- Compounds faster than switching

## Concept 4: Results Lag

### The 120-Day Reality

> "For every input you do - every YouTube video, ad campaign, cold email - most people go into the sales cycle. Let's say that's 120 days."

**What this means:**
- 120 days of videos
- 120 days of cold email
- 120 days of outreach
- ...before anything really takes place

### How Results Lag Hurts You (Two Ways)

**At the Start:**
> "A lot of people get too discouraged. They aren't consistent enough to even get through that 120 days. So they never see the outcome."

**At Scale:**
> "Now I'm generating 100 leads/month. I need to do operations, build the business, can't get more clients. So they decrease inputs or stop altogether."

**The Delayed Destruction:**
- At first, no real difference
- 30 days pass... okay
- 60 days pass... hmm
- 90 days pass... pipeline is DEAD
- Then they panic
- Go hard on inputs again
- Repeat cycle

### The Solution

**Build systems + hire to maintain inputs while you scale operations.**

Never stop the inputs. Results lag means:
- Starting early is critical
- Stopping has delayed consequences
- Consistency beats intensity

## Concept 5: The Four Types of Leverage

> From Naval Ravikant

### Why Leverage Matters

> "This is why some people get 10,000x the result you do when you both have 24 hours in a day."

### 1. Media Leverage

**What it is:** Content that reaches unlimited people

**Examples:**
- YouTube videos
- Newsletters
- Short form content
- Podcasts
- Written content

**The math:**
> "I'm making this YouTube video. At minimum several hundred, maybe thousands will see it. I didn't have to get on a Zoom call with 1,000 people."

**Key:** Do it once, unlimited people see it. Better content = bigger audience = more reach.

### 2. Code Leverage

**What it is:** Technology that does work for you

**Examples:**
- Cold email software (send 50K emails automatically)
- Funnels (nurture without manual explanation)
- AI (automation of everything)
- CRMs and automation

**Key:** Code doesn't sleep, doesn't need salary, scales infinitely.

### 3. Capital Leverage

**What it is:** Money that multiplies

**Examples:**
- Paid ads ($1 in → $2+ out)
- Investments in growth
- Raised prices from inverted supply/demand

**The math:**
> "If you can have a paid ad funnel where you put $1 in and you get $2 out, that is leverage."

### 4. Labor Leverage

**What it is:** Other people's time and effort

**Examples:**
- Overseas labor ($3K/month → $10K value)
- Trained employees ($10K/month → $50K value from systems)
- Partnerships (100 partners = fruits of 100 people's efforts)

```

      ↓
```
