## 2. STAGE 1: SOLO ($0-$20K/mo)

### Focus

- Get clients
- Deliver results
- Build proof
- Learn what works

### Priorities

1. Sales and lead generation
2. Service delivery
3. Building case studies
4. Refining offer

### Key Metrics

- Monthly revenue
- Client count
- Close rate
- Retention

### Exit Criteria

- Consistent $15-20K/month
- 3+ months stability
- Proven delivery system
- Time is the bottleneck

## 3. STAGE 2: FIRST HIRE ($20-$50K/mo)

### Focus

- Leverage through people
- Document processes
- Maintain quality
- Keep selling

### First Hire Options

**Delivery**: Someone to do the work
**Sales**: Someone to book/close calls
**Operations**: Someone to handle admin

**Recommendation**: Usually delivery firstâ€”free yourself to sell.

> **ðŸ“Š CROSS-REFERENCE/STANDARDIZED: First Hire Timing (FIX #25)**
>
> First hire depends on **business model**. Use this decision tree:
>
> | Business Model | First Hire | Revenue Trigger | Rationale |
> |----------------|------------|-----------------|-----------|
> | Service-heavy (fulfillment bottleneck) | Service Delivery | $15-20K/month | Free yourself to sell |
> | High-volume outreach (cold email, DMs) | Admin/Inbox Manager | Day 1 of outreach | Volume requires immediate support |
> | Sales-heavy (you are fulfillment) | Sales Support | $20K/month | You can't do both at scale |
> | Content-focused | Thumbnail/Video Editor | After 100 videos | Build skills first |
>
> **Key insight:** The $15-20K threshold applies to service delivery roles. Admin/inbox roles can come earlier if your model is volume-based.
>
> See also: `SKILL_BIBLE_agency_scaling_roadmap.md` - Detailed hiring sequence with specific triggers.

### Priorities

1. Hiring right person
2. Creating SOPs
3. Training and onboarding
4. Quality control
5. Continued sales

### Key Metrics

- Revenue per employee
- Client satisfaction
- Your time freed
- Employee performance

### Exit Criteria

- $40-50K/month
- First hire successful
- Core processes documented
- Ready for more leverage

### Priorities

## 6. COMMON SCALING MISTAKES

### Mistake 1: Hiring Too Late

**Problem**: Burning out before getting help
**Fix**: Hire when you can afford it, not when you're desperate

### Mistake 2: Hiring Too Fast

**Problem**: Adding overhead before revenue supports it
**Fix**: Hire based on demand, not ambition

### Mistake 3: No Systems

**Problem**: Everything depends on you
**Fix**: Document processes before/as you hire

### Mistake 4: Wrong Hires

**Problem**: Hiring for skill, not fit
**Fix**: Culture and attitude first, skills can be trained

### Mistake 5: Founder Bottleneck

**Problem**: Can't let go of tasks
**Fix**: Deliberately remove yourself from operations

## 7. SCALING LEVERS

### Revenue Levers

1. More clients (acquisition)
2. Higher prices (value)
3. More per client (expansion)
4. Longer retention (LTV)

### Efficiency Levers

1. Better systems (reduce waste)
2. Better tools (automate)
3. Better people (quality)
4. Better processes (optimize)

### Which to Pull When

- Early: Focus on acquisition
- Mid: Focus on pricing and systems
- Later: Focus on retention and expansion

---

## 8. FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT

### Key Financial Metrics

- Revenue
- Gross margin (after delivery costs)
- Net margin (after all costs)
- Cash flow
- Runway

### Healthy Margins

- 50-70% gross margin
- 20-30% net margin
- 3+ months runway

### When to Invest

- Proven ROI on investment
- Stable foundation
- Clear growth path
- Not just "hoping"
