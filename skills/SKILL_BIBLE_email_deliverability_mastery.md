## PART 1: FOUNDATIONAL CONCEPTS

### Core Philosophy

Max's fundamental belief is that email deliverability can be summarized in 10 seconds: "It's your engagement optimization. Getting people to open and click your emails." Everything elseâ€”technical setup, warming periods, segmentationâ€”serves this single purpose of proving to inbox providers that real people want to receive and engage with your emails.

The philosophy centers on building what Max calls your "domain credit score"â€”similar to a financial credit score, but for email sending. Just as banks trust you with money based on your financial history, inbox providers trust you with inbox placement based on your sending reputation. This reputation is built through consistent engagement patterns, proper technical setup, and gradual volume increases that prove you're a legitimate sender.

Max emphasizes that deliverability is not a set-it-and-forget-it system. It requires constant monitoring, adjustment, and optimization. The inbox landscape changes rapidly, with providers like Gmail implementing new filtering mechanisms regularly. Success comes from understanding the underlying principles rather than relying on specific tactics that may become obsolete.

### Key Principles

**1. Engagement is Everything**
Your open rates, click rates, and reply rates are the holy grail metrics that directly tell inbox providers whether people want your emails. Max targets minimum 50% open rates and 5% click rates. Anything below these benchmarks signals to providers that your emails may be unwanted.

**2. Technical Setup is Non-Negotiable**
DMARC, CNAME, and TXT records must be properly configured. Max specifically recommends using tools like Glock Apps to verify your technical setup. Missing or incorrect technical configuration can immediately flag your domain as untrustworthy.

**3. Warming is Mandatory for New Domains**
Never send to large lists immediately with a new domain or IP. Max's warming process starts with 200-400 people and gradually increases by 50-100% each send, monitoring engagement metrics at each stage.

**4. Consistency Builds Trust**
Inbox providers look for consistent sending patterns. Max recommends sending at least 2-3 emails per week to maintain visibility with providers. Sporadic sending patterns appear spammy and hurt deliverability.

**5. Segmentation Prevents Reputation Damage**
Sending to unengaged subscribers destroys your reputation. Max uses engagement windowsâ€”only sending to people who have engaged within specific timeframes.

**6. Volume Scaling Must Be Gradual**
Sudden volume spikes trigger spam filters. Max's approach involves careful volume increases, monitoring engagement at each level before expanding further.

**7. Content Quality Affects Algorithmic Scoring**
While engagement matters most, content still impacts deliverability. Max avoids spam trigger words like "sale," "free trial," and excessive capitalization, especially in subject lines.

**8. Unsubscribe Rates Are Critical Indicators**
Max targets unsubscribe rates below 0.1%. Higher rates signal to providers that recipients don't want your emails, directly impacting future deliverability.

**9. Spam Complaint Rates Must Stay Minimal**
Complaint rates above 0.5% cause serious deliverability issues. Max monitors this metric closely and adjusts strategy immediately if rates climb.

**10. Provider-Specific Optimization**
Different providers (Gmail, Yahoo, Outlook) have different filtering mechanisms. Max tracks performance across providers and adjusts strategies accordingly.

**11. Automation Flows Protect Reputation**
High-engagement flows (welcome, abandoned cart, post-purchase) help maintain overall account reputation even when campaign performance fluctuates.

**12. Monitoring Tools Are Essential**
Max uses Glock Apps for deliverability testing and Klaviyo's native reporting for engagement tracking. Regular monitoring catches issues before they become critical.

### The Framework Overview

Max's deliverability framework operates on three interconnected levels:

**Foundation Level:** Technical setup and domain configuration that establishes basic trust with inbox providers.

**Engagement Level:** Content and segmentation strategies that drive the engagement metrics providers use to determine reputation.

**Optimization Level:** Ongoing monitoring, testing, and adjustment based on performance data and provider algorithm changes.

The framework recognizes that deliverability is both a technical and marketing challenge. Technical setup gets you in the game, but engagement optimization determines whether you win or lose. The system is designed to be sustainable long-term, building genuine engagement rather than trying to manipulate provider algorithms.

## PART 2: STRATEGIC FRAMEWORK

### Step-by-Step Process

**Phase 1: Technical Foundation Setup (Days 1-3)**

1. **Domain Configuration**
   - Set up DMARC records through your domain provider
   - Configure CNAME and TXT records as specified by your email platform
   - Verify all technical setup using Glock Apps (glockapps.com)
   - Ensure SPF and DKIM records are properly aligned

2. **Platform Integration**
   - Connect domain to email platform (Klaviyo recommended)
   - Verify domain ownership and authentication
   - Set up tracking and analytics integration
   - Configure unsubscribe handling and compliance features

3. **Initial Testing**
   - Run full spam test through Glock Apps
   - Send test emails to multiple provider types (Gmail, Yahoo, Outlook)
   - Verify emails land in primary inbox, not promotions or spam
   - Check all links, images, and tracking functionality

**Phase 2: List Segmentation and Preparation (Days 4-7)**

4. **Engagement Segmentation**
   - Create 90-day engaged segment (opened email, clicked, or active on site)
   - Create 30-day highly engaged segment for initial warming
   - Identify VIP customers and recent purchasers
   - Remove completely unengaged subscribers (no activity in 6+ months)

5. **Warming List Creation**
   - Start with 200-400 most engaged subscribers
   - Ensure this group has high open rates (60%+ historically)
   - Include recent customers and active website visitors
   - Exclude any subscribers with engagement issues

6. **Content Planning**
   - Plan 4-6 warming emails with valuable, non-promotional content
   - Prepare welcome flow for new subscribers during warming
   - Create abandoned cart and post-purchase flows
   - Develop regular campaign content calendar

**Phase 3: Warming Period Execution (Weeks 2-8)**

7. **Initial Sends (Week 2)**
   - Send to 200-400 most engaged subscribers
   - Target 60%+ open rates, 5%+ click rates
   - Monitor unsubscribe rates (keep below 0.1%)
   - Wait 3-4 days between sends

8. **Gradual Expansion (Weeks 3-4)**
   - Increase list size by 50-100% if engagement targets met
   - Continue monitoring engagement metrics closely
   - Adjust content if engagement drops below targets
   - Maintain consistent sending schedule

9. **Volume Scaling (Weeks 5-8)**
   - Continue gradual increases: 1,000 â†’ 2,500 â†’ 5,000 â†’ 10,000+
   - Monitor deliverability through Glock Apps weekly
   - Track provider-specific performance (Gmail, Yahoo, Outlook)
   - Adjust strategy based on performance data

**Phase 4: Full Operation (Week 9+)**

10. **Regular Campaign Schedule**
    - Send 2-3 campaigns per week to engaged segments
    - Monthly sends to broader list for re-engagement
    - Maintain consistent sending patterns
    - Continue monitoring and optimization

### Timing & Sequencing

**Warming Schedule:**
- Week 1: Technical setup and testing
- Week 2: 200-400 subscribers, 2 sends
- Week 3: 400-800 subscribers, 2 sends  
- Week 4: 800-1,500 subscribers, 2 sends
- Week 5: 1,500-2,500 subscribers, 3 sends
- Week 6: 2,500-5,000 subscribers, 3 sends
- Week 7: 5,000-10,000 subscribers, 3 sends
- Week 8+: Full list operation, 3-4 sends per week

**Critical Timing Rules:**
- Wait 3-4 days between warming sends
- Never increase volume if engagement drops below 50% opens
- Allow 24-48 hours to assess engagement before next send
- Pause warming if unsubscribe rates exceed 0.1%

**Seasonal Considerations:**
Max notes that major sales events (Black Friday, etc.) require special handling:
- Increase warming timeline before major events
- Pre-warm with highly engaged segments
- Monitor deliverability more closely during high-volume periods
- Have backup segments ready if primary lists show fatigue

### Segmentation Strategy

**Primary Segments:**

**90-Day Engaged (Primary Revenue Driver)**
- Opened email in past 90 days, OR
- Clicked email in past 90 days, OR
- Active on website in past 90 days, OR
- Made purchase in past 90 days
- This segment receives 80% of regular campaigns

**30-Day Highly Engaged (Warming and Testing)**
- Multiple opens/clicks in past 30 days
- Recent purchasers
- High lifetime value customers
- Used for warming new domains and testing new content

**VIP/Recent Customers**
- Purchased within past 30 days
- High lifetime value (top 20% of customers)
- Receives exclusive content and early access
- Highest engagement rates, protects overall reputation

**Re-engagement Candidates**
- No activity in 90-180 days
- Previously engaged subscribers
- Receives monthly re-engagement campaigns
- Removed if no response after 3 attempts

**Suppression Lists:**
- No activity in 180+ days
- Multiple unsubscribes/complaints
- Invalid email addresses
- Competitors and spam traps (identified through monitoring)

**Geographic/Provider Segmentation:**
Max tracks performance by:
- Email provider (Gmail, Yahoo, Outlook, Apple)
- Geographic location (US, international)
- Device type (mobile vs desktop)
- Engagement patterns (time of day, frequency preferences)

### A/B Testing Framework

**What to Test During Warming:**
- Subject line styles (questions vs statements)
- Sender names (founder vs company)
- Send times (morning vs evening)
- Content length (short vs long)

**What NOT to Test During Warming:**
- Dramatic design changes
- Completely different content types
- Frequency changes
- Large segment variations

**Testing Methodology:**
1. Test one variable at a time
2. Use minimum 1,000 recipients per variation
3. Wait 24 hours before declaring winner
4. Implement winner for next send
5. Document results for future reference

**Advanced Testing (Post-Warming):**

- Dynamic content vs static
- Interactive elements
- Video vs image vs text

## PART 6: COMMON MISTAKES & SOLUTIONS

### Critical Errors to Avoid

**The "Blast to Everyone" Mistake:**
*Problem:* Sending to entire list without segmentation
*Consequence:* Immediate deliverability damage, high unsubscribe rates
*Solution:* Always segment by engagement, start with 90-day engaged only

**The "New Domain Mass Send" Mistake:**
*Problem:* Sending to large lists immediately with new domain
*Consequence:* Domain reputation damage, spam folder placement
*Solution:* Follow Max's warming protocol: 200 â†’ 400 â†’ 800 â†’ 1,500 progression

**The "Ignore Technical Setup" Mistake:**
*Problem:* Skipping DMARC, SPF, DKIM configuration
*Consequence:* Automatic spam filtering, authentication failures
*Solution:* Complete technical setup before any sending, verify with Glock Apps

**The "Inconsistent Sending" Mistake:**
*Problem:* Sporadic email sending (once per month or irregular)
*Consequence:* Loss of inbox provider trust, decreased deliverability
*Solution:* Maintain 2-3 emails per week minimum, consistent schedule

**The "Promotional Overload" Mistake:**
*Problem:* Every email is a sales pitch or promotion
*Consequence:* Subscriber fatigue, high unsubscribe rates, spam complaints
*Solution:* 80% value content, 20% promotional content ratio

### Troubleshooting Guide

**Problem: Open Rates Suddenly Drop Below 30%**
*Diagnosis Steps:*
1. Check Glock Apps for deliverability issues
2. Review recent subject lines for spam triggers
3. Analyze sending volume changes
4. Check for technical configuration changes

*Solutions:*
- Reduce sending volume by 50%
- Switch to highly engaged segments only
- Implement re-engagement campaign
- Review and clean technical setup

**Problem: High Unsubscribe Rates (Above 0.5%)**
*Diagnosis Steps:*
1. Review recent email content for aggressive sales language
2. Check sending frequency increases
3. Analyze subscriber acquisition sources
4. Review segmentation accuracy

*Solutions:*
- Reduce sending frequency
- Implement preference center
- Improve content value proposition
- Tighten list acquisition standards

**Problem: Emails Landing in Promotions Tab**
*Diagnosis Steps:*
1. Check for promotional language in subject lines
2. Review sender name consistency
3. Analyze content structure and CTAs
4. Test different content types

*Solutions:*
- Use personal sender names (founder/team member)
- Reduce promotional language
- Increase text-based, personal content
- Encourage subscribers to move emails to primary

**Problem: Spam Folder Placement**
*Diagnosis Steps:*
1. Run comprehensive Glock Apps test
2. Check domain reputation scores
3. Review recent content for spam triggers
4. Analyze engagement metrics trends

*Solutions:*
- Immediately reduce sending volume
- Send only to most engaged subscribers
- Clean up technical configuration
- Implement gradual reputation rebuilding

**Problem: Low Click Rates Despite Good Opens**
*Diagnosis Steps:*
1. Review CTA placement and design
2. Check mobile optimization
3. Analyze content relevance
4. Test different offer types

*Solutions:*
- Simplify CTA design and placement
- Improve mobile experience
- Increase content personalization
- A/B test different value propositions
