# Pre-Call Research

## Purpose
Conduct rapid, focused research before sales calls to personalize your approach, anticipate objections, and demonstrate expertise from minute one.

## When to Use
- Before every scheduled discovery call
- Before follow-up calls with warm leads
- Before demo or presentation calls
- When prospect books through your calendar
- Before high-value prospect conversations

## Core Philosophy

**5 minutes of research = 10X better call.**

Most sellers wing it. Elite closers spend 5-10 minutes researching each prospect and use that intel to:
- Build instant rapport (common ground)
- Ask better questions (specific, not generic)
- Anticipate objections (prepare responses)
- Demonstrate competence (know their world)
- Stand out (everyone else is generic)

Your job: Know more about them than they expect you to.

## The 5-Minute Research Framework

### Time Allocation:
```
1 min: Review booking form answers
2 min: Check their website/online presence
1 min: Scan LinkedIn
1 min: Look for recent news/activity
```

Total: 5 minutes, maximum ROI.

## Advanced Research Tactics

### For High-Value Prospects (10-15 Minutes)

If it's a dream client or high ticket, go deeper:

#### 1. Social Media Deep Dive

**Twitter/X:**
- Recent tweets (what they care about)
- Who they follow (influences)
- Tone (formal vs casual)

**Instagram (if relevant):**
- Personal interests
- Lifestyle (helps with rapport)

**YouTube:**
- Do they have a channel?
- What topics do they cover?
- Watch one video for context

#### 2. Podcast Appearances

Search: "[Their Name] podcast"

Listen to 10-15 minutes for:
- How they think
- Their communication style
- Pain points they mention
- Goals they discuss

#### 3. Competitor Analysis

Who are their main competitors?

```
Google: "alternatives to [their company]"
Google: "competitors to [their company]"
```

Understand their competitive landscape = better positioning.

#### 4. Tech Stack (For Tech-Savvy Businesses)

Use tools like:
- BuiltWith.com (what tech they use)
- Wappalyzer (browser extension)

Tells you:
- What tools they're already using
- Integrations they need
- Budget level (expensive tools = higher budget)

#### 5. Glassdoor/Reviews

If they have employees:
- Check Glassdoor for company culture
- Check Google Reviews for customer sentiment

Red flags = internal issues or customer problems.

## Research-Based Opening Lines

Use your research to create custom openers:

### Website-Based:

```
"I was checking out your site before this call - I saw you work with [their market]. How long have you been focused on that niche?"
```

### LinkedIn-Based:

```
"I noticed you've been [Role] for [X] years - what made you get into [industry]?"
```

### Recent Activity-Based:

```
"I saw you recently [launched X / posted about Y] - that's awesome. How's it going so far?"
```

### Common Connection-Based:

```
"I saw we're both connected to [Name] - how do you know them?"
```

### Location-Based:

```
"I saw you're based in [City] - I actually [visited there / worked with several businesses there / have family there]. How's the business scene there?"
```

**Why These Work:**

- Show you did homework (respect)
- Specific (not generic)
- Easy to answer (comfortable)
- Build rapport quickly

## What to Do With Research During Call

### Phase 1: Rapport Building

```
"Before we dive in, I have to say - I was looking at [specific thing from research].

[Ask question about it]

How'd you get into [industry/role]?"
```

Use research to break ice and show interest.

### Phase 2: Discovery

```
"So you mentioned in the form that [their answer].

Tell me more about that - what's going on there?"
```

Reference their booking form to show you read it.

### Phase 3: Objection Handling

If you anticipated objection from research:

```
"I imagine you've tried [thing you saw on their site/LinkedIn].

What happened with that?"
```

Shows you understand their context.

### Phase 4: Closing

```
"Based on what you've told me and what I saw with [specific research point], I think [solution] would be perfect for your situation.

Here's why..."
```

Tie back to research for personalized close.

## The Golden Rule

**Research is a tool for connection, not interrogation.**

Use it to:
- Show respect (you cared enough to look)
- Build rapport (common ground)
- Ask better questions (specific, not generic)
- Stand out (everyone else is lazy)

Don't use it to:
- Show off how much you know
- Overwhelm them with details
- Make assumptions about their business
- Skip actual discovery

Research informs discovery. It doesn't replace it.
